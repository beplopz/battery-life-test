Topic 15 • Battery Life Test

Measuring laptop battery life across usage scenarios

A visual guide for testing laptop battery performance, comparing power consumption with a desktop computer, and turning observations into a clear, evidence-based report.

Usage scenarios Battery runtime Power consumption

Objective

What this website shows

Partners can measure how long a laptop lasts under common activities such as idle work, browsing, video playback, gaming, and productivity tasks. The results are compared against a desktop computer's power draw to explain energy efficiency and practical use.

Deliverable 1

Battery life test flowchart

Follow these steps to collect consistent runtime and power-use data.

1

Prepare devices

Fully charge the laptop, plug in the desktop, close unnecessary apps, and set matching brightness.

2

Select scenarios

Choose idle, web browsing, video playback, productivity, and high-performance workload tests.

3

Run each test

Start the timer, perform the selected activity, and monitor the laptop battery percentage.

4

Record readings

Log elapsed time, remaining battery, laptop settings, and desktop wattage from a power meter.

5

Compare results

Calculate energy use, compare laptop runtime with desktop power draw, and identify efficient scenarios.

6

Conclude

Summarize which tasks drain the battery fastest and recommend settings that extend battery life.

Deliverable 3

Sample comparison table

Use this format to record actual class results and compare energy demand across scenarios.

Usage scenario Laptop battery change Estimated laptop runtime Desktop power consumption Observation
Idle / standby Slow drain 8–12 hours 45–80 W Best case for laptop battery life.
Web browsing Moderate drain 5–8 hours 80–140 W Brightness and open tabs affect results.
Video playback Moderate drain 4–7 hours 90–160 W Efficient playback uses less energy than gaming.
Productivity apps Medium to fast drain 3–6 hours 100–180 W Office tasks vary based on multitasking.
Gaming / heavy load Fast drain 1–3 hours 200–500 W CPU and GPU demand sharply increases power use.
